A simple study report in the cybersecurity area

October 15, 2019 earth_diqiu Comments 0 Comment

Background

Let’s start with a traditional assumption in the cybersecurity area: Alice and Bob want to exchange a message. How could they ensure Confidentiality, Integrity, and Non-repudiation exchange their message. (I’ll skip the topic about Availability since I think it’s not very relevant with the main area of this paragraph)

methods they could choose

PGP-Pretty Good Privacy

Just as its name, PGP is a famous encryption program developed by Phil Zimmermann in 1991[source]. After about 20 years, it’s derivative work, like GnuPG, still active in the field of email encryption. This is a quick overview of how it works.

To make this program work, Alice and Bob should share their Public key, typically it’s an RSA key.

As shown in the picture, by combining asymmetric encryption algorithms, symmetric encryption algorithms, and hash functions, PGP can guarantee message integrity, confidentiality, and non-repudiation.

Since the effect of PGP is so good, it’s developer, Phil Zimmermann, were investigated by the US government on the grounds of “export arms.”

However, nowadays, people have found some weaknesses in it, like it doesn’t have forward secrecy and can’t resist censorship.

TLS – Transport Layer Security

Whats if, Bob, is a website?

The answer is TLS, a well-known, widely used, constantly updated transport layer security protocol. Not only can it be used on web security, but also developers are able to use this protocol to protect their internet traffic in any C/S model.

How to implement TLS?

The most popular open-source lib are as follow.

It is widely used by Internet servers, including the majority of HTTPS websites.[source]
It is managed by the OpenSSL management committee.
License: Apache License 2.0

One famous software bug – Heartbleed – affected and affecting a great number of websites, based on its popularity. This bug caused by buffer over-reading and could lead to sensitive information leaks. This bug pulled into its source code on December 31, 2011,[source] and be fixed on April 7th, 2014.(OpenSSL 1.0.1g)

mbed TLS (formerly known as PolarSSL) makes it trivially easy for developers to include cryptographic and SSL/TLS capabilities in their (embedded) products, facilitating this functionality with a minimal coding footprint.[source]
It is managed by ARM Limited.
License: Apache 2.0 license or the GPL 2.0 license.

The wolfSSL embedded SSL library is a lightweight, portable, C-language-based SSL/TLS library targeted at IoT, embedded, and RTOS environments primarily because of its size, speed, and feature set.[source]
It is managed by wolfSSL Inc.
License: GPLv2 or commercial license.

wolfSSL is the first commercial TLS 1.3 implementation

Other Choices?

If one is trying to DIY his or her own encryption protocol?

Sodium is a modern, easy-to-use software library for encryption, decryption, signatures, password hashing and more.[source]

It is managed by libsodium.org
license: ISC license.

If one is trying to deal with censorship issues?

A proxy protocol that helps you to bypass firewall build by ISP, company, and government. The main focus of this project is hiding users’ internet traffic. It supports multiple protocols, like including Socks, HTTP, Shadowsocks, VMess.[source]

Obfuscation

The most interest concept in cybersecurity that I didn’t mention is obfuscation. If we can say that the significance of encryption is to ensure that information is not tampered with, not known to third parties, etc; the significance of obfuscation is to ensure third parties are not able to guess who I’m communicating. The opposite of the former is that we have to leak some information on purpose in order to make a censorship program think we are contacting someone we are allowed.

By the developing of machine learning and AI technology, obfuscation algorithm, such as obfs3 & obfs4(both is developed by Tor project), should be uploaded, to face the challenge of rapidly improving Deep packet inspection method.
